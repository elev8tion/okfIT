# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

okfit is an open-source project for turning knowledge sources into portable OKF bundles and queryable MCP memory. The project should be useful, technical, and welcoming to contributors with different backgrounds and experience levels.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful, direct, and constructive.
- Assume good intent, but correct wrong technical claims with evidence.
- Keep feedback focused on code, docs, behavior, reproducibility, and project goals.
- Accept maintainer decisions about scope, architecture, and release quality.
- Credit ideas, reports, examples, and fixes from other contributors.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, stalking, or sustained disruption.
- Insults, slurs, sexualized language, or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Public or private sharing of personal information without permission.
- Bad-faith arguments, spam, impersonation, or deliberate misinformation.
- Publishing private docs, credentials, tokens, cookies, or exploit details.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, reject contributions, block accounts, or restrict participation when behavior harms the project or community.

Reports can be made to project maintainers through GitHub. If privacy is needed and the repository has no private contact channel yet, open a minimal public issue asking maintainers to establish one, without including sensitive details.

Maintainers should handle reports promptly, fairly, and with respect for reporter privacy. Retaliation against someone who reports a concern is unacceptable.

## Scope

This code applies in project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, releases, community chat, social posts representing the project, and maintainer interactions related to okfit.
